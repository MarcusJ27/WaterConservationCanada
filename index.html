Welcome to Water Conservation Canada

Canada has over two million lakes and rivers, and more inland waters than any other country. These waters flow to all three coasts, crossing international, provincial, and territorial boundaries. For many Indigenous Peoples, water is sacred, protected, and honoured as the giver of all life. Fresh water is also central to economic growth, environmental health, including supporting biodiversity, and the wellbeing of Canadians.

Join Us in Protecting Canada's Water Resources

Water Conservation Canada is dedicated to preserving and protecting Canada's water resources. Our mission is to raise awareness about water conservation and advocate for sustainable water management practices. Together, we can ensure clean and safe water for current and future generations.

Featured Projects

  • River Cleanup Campaign: Removing pollutants and debris from local rivers.
  • Water Quality Monitoring: Collaborating with communities to monitor water quality in lakes and streams.
  • Water Conservation Education: Providing educational programs to schools and communities on water conservation practices.

Tips for Cleaner Water

  • Reduce, reuse, and recycle waste.
  • Avoid using harmful chemicals and pesticides.
  • Conserve water by fixing leaks and using water-efficient fixtures.
  • Dispose of hazardous materials properly.
  • Participate in local river or beach cleanups.
  • Use biodegradable cleaning products.
  • Plant trees and shrubs to reduce runoff.
  • Avoid flushing medications down the toilet.
  • Support policies and practices that protect water resources.
  • Educate others about the importance of water conservation.
